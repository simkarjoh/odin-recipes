Meatballs

Description

Meatballs are a classic and versatile dish enjoyed in many cultures around the world. Typically made from ground meat, such as beef, pork, chicken, or a combination, they are seasoned with herbs, spices, breadcrumbs, and sometimes cheese or eggs to enhance their flavor and texture. Meatballs can be baked, fried, or simmered in sauces, making them adaptable to a variety of cuisines—from Italian meatballs served with marinara sauce over pasta, to Swedish meatballs with a creamy gravy, or even spiced meatballs in Middle Eastern dishes. They are comforting, flavorful, and easy to prepare, making them a favorite for family dinners and gatherings.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1/2 lb ground pork
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried oregano

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine ground beef, ground pork, breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, minced garlic, chopped parsley, egg, salt, pepper, and oregano.
  3. Mix the ingredients together until well combined, but do not overmix.
  4. Shape the mixture into small balls, about 1-2 inches in diameter.
  5. Place the meatballs on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper or lightly greased.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the meatballs are cooked through and browned.
  7. Remove the meatballs from the oven and let them rest for a few minutes.
  8. Serve the meatballs with your favorite sauce or as part of a dish like spaghetti or subs.
